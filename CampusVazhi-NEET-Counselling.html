COUNSELLING ROUNDS

The 4 Rounds, Explained Without Jargon

Parents: read this twice. Each round has irreversible choices.

1
Round 1
~6 weeks after NEET result

Register, fill choices (100+ allowed), lock. Allotment published. Accept, report to college, or opt for upgrade.

  • Choice filling is the MOST important step
  • "Upgrade" keeps current seat + tries better
  • "Fresh" surrenders current seat entirely
2
Round 2
~2 weeks after Round 1

Vacant seats + upgraded seats. Second chance to improve. Can add or modify choices based on Round 1 cutoffs.

  • Updated cutoffs published — study them
  • Resignation window closes after this
  • Seat matrix expands for new colleges
3
Mop-Up Round
~1 week after Round 2

Last round under MCC/state authority. Mostly private and deemed seats. High stakes, high confusion.

  • Most deemed university seats open here
  • Financial commitment becomes real
  • Read fee structure carefully before locking
4
Stray Vacancy
~10 days after Mop-Up

College-level filling under state control. Absolute last chance for a medical seat in the cycle.

  • College-level allocation, not centralized
  • Travel to college personally may be required
  • Fraud risk — verify agent intermediaries
DECISION FRAMEWORK

MBBS vs BDS vs AYUSH — The Honest Tradeoffs

Your score forces a choice. Here's what each path actually looks like 10 years out.

FIRST CHOICE

MBBS

5.5 years · Govt or private · NEET PG door
Why choose it
  • Widest practice scope
  • NEET PG gives 60+ specialty paths
  • Government jobs, rural service
  • Highest social trust
Tradeoffs
  • Private fees 10-80 lakhs/yr
  • Long hours, high stress
  • PG another 3-year grind
STRONG SECOND

BDS

5 years · Dental · Good private practice
Why choose it
  • Private clinic = own business
  • Shorter, clearer path
  • Work-life balance better than MBBS
  • Growing cosmetic dentistry market
Tradeoffs
  • Saturated in metros
  • Clinic setup ₹15-30 lakhs
  • Insurance doesn't cover most work
CONSIDER CAREFULLY

AYUSH

5.5 years · BAMS/BHMS/BUMS/BSMS
Why choose it
  • Lower fees at govt colleges
  • Integrated wellness is growing
  • Government AYUSH jobs available
  • MD AYUSH paths exist
Tradeoffs
  • Scope-of-practice debates
  • Lower social prestige vs MBBS
  • Private practice hardest to build
