How to Prepare

A structured month-by-month roadmap to crack this exam

1
Know Your Post â PG Assistant vs BT Assistant
PG Assistant requires a Post-Graduate degree + B.Ed; BT Assistant requires a Graduate degree + B.Ed. Polytechnic Lecturer needs M.E/M.Tech. Confirm your eligibility before applying.
2
Master Your Subject Paper
The subject paper (Physics, Maths, Tamil, etc.) carries the majority of marks. Use your degree syllabus as the base and go 2 levels deeper for competitive questions.
3
Educational Psychology & Pedagogy
All TRB exams include a Pedagogy section. Topics: Child Development, Learning Theories, Inclusive Education, Assessment â score well here to separate yourself.
4
General Knowledge & Current Affairs
Around 20â25% of the paper covers GK, Tamil Nadu current events, and education policy. Daily 30-minute reading of a newspaper covers this adequately.
5
Previous Year Papers â 10 Years
TRB follows patterns. Solve the last 10 years' papers for your subject. Identify repeated topics and high-weightage areas. Pattern analysis = strategic preparation.
6
Time Management in the Exam Hall
TRB exams are 3 hours with 150 questions. Target 1.2 min/question. Attempt subject paper first (your strength), then GK, and leave difficult questions for last.
