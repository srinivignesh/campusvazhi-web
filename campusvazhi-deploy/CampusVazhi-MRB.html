How to Prepare

A structured month-by-month roadmap to crack this exam

1
Confirm Your Post & Qualification
MRB recruits for Staff Nurse (B.Sc Nursing), Lab Technician (DMLT/B.Sc MLT), Pharmacist (D.Pharm/B.Pharm), Physiotherapist, and more. Match your qualification to the correct post before preparing.
2
Subject-Specific Syllabus Deep Dive
The written exam is primarily from your professional curriculum. For Nurses: Anatomy, Physiology, Medical-Surgical Nursing, OBG, Community Nursing. For Lab Technicians: Clinical Biochemistry, Microbiology, Haematology.
3
General Knowledge & Current Health Affairs
MRB exams include a GK section covering Tamil Nadu health schemes (Makkalai Thedi Maruthuvam, CMCHIS), national health policies, and general science. 20–30 marks typically.
4
Previous Year Papers (MRB + Allied Boards)
MRB papers repeat concept areas. Solve last 5 years' papers for your post. Also refer to AIIMS/JIPMER nursing exam papers for clinical question patterns.
5
Speed + Accuracy for MCQ Stage
MRB written tests are MCQ-based (100–150 questions, 2 hours). Practice solving under time pressure — 45 seconds per question is the benchmark. Mark review questions and come back.
6
Practical / Skill Assessment (Some Posts)
Certain posts include a practical stage. Refresh your clinical skills, ensure you can demonstrate procedures correctly, and review your hospital training notes from your course.
